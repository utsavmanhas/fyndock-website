GST ITC reconciliation for a multi-entity VC fund — Oct–Dec 2025
The Problem
ITC mismatch between GSTR-2B portal data and internal ledger across multiple quarters. The fund had eligible credits it wasn't claiming — and was at risk of a GST notice for incorrect credit assertion.
What We Did
Built a 6-sheet reconciliation workbook covering Oct–Dec 2025. Matched every invoice to the portal, flagged RCM transactions, identified mismatches by vendor, and built a prioritised action tracker for resolution.
The Result
Full reconciliation completed. ₹4.2L of eligible ITC confirmed and claimed. Compliance risk resolved before assessment period. Fund's finance team now runs the same template monthly.
Outcome
Full reconciliation completed. Eligible ITC confirmed and claimed. Compliance risk resolved before assessment — with a repeatable monthly process now in place.
EduTech

Unit Economics

Department-level P&L and unit economics for a mid-size EduTech company
The Problem
Finance team had zero visibility into which programs were profitable. Costs were lumped into a single overhead bucket. All strategic decisions — which programs to scale, which to shut — were being made on intuition.
What We Did
Built granular unit economics from scratch — cost attribution by department, program stream, batch, and student headcount. Separated direct and indirect costs for the first time. Built a template the finance team could own going forward.
The Result
Leadership saw for the first time which programs were subsidising which. Three departments were restructured within one quarter based directly on the unit economics output.
Outcome
First-ever program-level profitability view. Three departments restructured within one quarter. Decision-making moved from intuition to data.

Monthly MIS and Virtual CFO for a funded D2C brand — channel-wise profitability
The Problem
Founder was receiving a Tally printout monthly. No channel-wise gross margins. No understanding of true profitability after marketplace fees, returns, and logistics. The highest-revenue SKU could have been loss-making for months.
What We Did
Designed a monthly MIS with channel-wise revenue, returns-adjusted net revenue, platform fee attribution, and contribution margin by SKU. Built SKU-level P&L for the top 20 products — separating marketplace, own-website, and quick-commerce channels.
The Result
The highest-revenue SKU had a −3.0% contribution margin post-returns and marketplace fees. Product line restructured. 22% improvement in blended margins across the portfolio within two quarters.
Outcome
Highest-revenue SKU revealed as margin-negative. Product line restructured. 22% improvement in blended contribution margins within 2 quarters.
